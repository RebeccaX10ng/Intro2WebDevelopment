"We Can Do Better"

Many websites are providing great language supports, which can even be better with the support of related communities.

Wikipedia

Up to now, Wikipedia is available in more than 300 languages. As a non-profit website, there are many communities all over the world editing content in their own languages and upload it to Wikipedia. The open and friendly community atmosphere and its popularity is attracting more people to take part in this activity.

Netflix

Netflix provides multilingual services through a variety of means to cater to its global audience. As it has expanded into nearly every country worldwide, the platform has developed features like multilingual subs and dubs that allow viewers to access content in multiple languages.

TED

TED, the global well-known education platform, provides Open Translation Project that allows volunteers to translate TED Talks into a wide range of languages. This community-driven initiative has enabled TED to provide subtitles and translated content in over 100 languages. Volunteers work from around the world to translate, review, and ensure high-quality subtitles for the talks.

Steam

Steam, world's most famous PC game platform, attracts game players and producers from all over the world. It fully supports 29 languages and partly supports more than 100 languages. Many games on Steam allows community translation MODs, which enables local communities to translate their favourite games and then share them with other players.
